OUR PROPRIETARY GAMING APP
aRCadeRally
is powered by our proprietary app, available both through the web browser and as a desktop installation for Windows and macOS.
Players use one connected account with access to their profile, bookings, session history, statistics, and wallet, where they can top up funds and prepare for the next race.
Our platform combines real-time video, live control, and a full gameplay interface built for REAL-WORLD GAMING, giving players a smooth experience whether they play online or through the installed desktop app.
Cars are controlled using a keyboard and gamepad, and in the future we also plan to add steering wheel support for an even more immersive experience.
PROOF OF REAL INTERNET CONTROL
aRCadeRally is not just an idea on paper.
These videos show our real early-stage remote driving system already working in practice.
In our test sessions, real RC cars are driven over the internet from different locations, using live control and real physical movement. One vehicle is controlled with a keyboard, while another is controlled with a gamepad from a different place, proving that the core experience is already possible in the real world.
These are not simulated scenes or visual mockups. They are real test drives that show the foundation of what aRCadeRally is being built on: remote control, real vehicles, real terrain, and real gameplay potential.
This is exactly why we believe aRCadeRally can grow into a true REAL-WORLD GAMING platform — because the key principle has already been tested in reality.

OUR FIRST TRACK: aRCade One
Our first flagship location, aRCade One, is being built as a real off-road track designed for fast, technical, and spectacular driving.
Sandy terrain, jumps, bumps, changing grip, and outdoor conditions make every session dynamic and different.
And this is only the beginning.
aRCadeRally is designed to grow into a network of tracks in many different environments — outdoor arenas, warehouses, halls, and other unique locations — all connected through one central platform.

BUILT FOR REAL-WORLD GAMING
The vehicles in aRCadeRally are not random RC toys placed on a track.
Each car must be suitable for repeated online gameplay, onboard camera use, stable control, serviceability, and long-term operation inside the aRCadeRally ecosystem.
The RC model is only the base.
We select, adapt, improve, and develop vehicles so they work as real gameplay machines, not just hobby products.
PROPRIETARY TECHNOLOGY
Behind the experience is proprietary technology built specifically for REAL-WORLD GAMING.
aRCadeRally combines live video, real-time control, booking systems, player accounts, rankings, session logic, and scalable track management into one ecosystem.
This is not a one-time attraction.
It is a platform designed to scale from one flagship track into a global network of partner-operated locations with centralized bookings, payments, and region-based availability.
WHY THIS MATTERS
aRCadeRally creates a new category at the intersection of gaming, motorsport, RC hobby, and live internet entertainment.
It combines the accessibility of gaming with the authenticity of the physical world.
That is why we call it REAL-WORLD GAMING.
WE ARE NOT STARTING FROM ZERO
We already have the concept, the brand, the first track under construction, real vehicle tests, a growing community, and a product architecture designed for real deployment.
The next stage is about turning this foundation into a fully operational platform that players can use to drive, compete, and experience an entirely new kind of gameplay.
PROOF OF EXPERIENCE
aRCadeRally is not coming from theory only.
It is being built on real technical, operational, and content experience gathered through earlier engineering work, off-road projects, and real audience validation.
Before building aRCadeRally, we were already creating real machines, working on remote-control concepts, building our own off-road environment, and testing how strong real-world motorsport content performs online. This matters because aRCadeRally is not just about an idea — it requires execution across hardware, software, track operations, and audience building.
