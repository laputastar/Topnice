Risks and challenges
Every HAMON knife is produced through a multi-step manufacturing process involving layered Damascus steel forging, blade grinding, black titanium surface finishing, assembly, inspection, and final packaging.

Because the blade combines Damascus steel construction with a titanium-coated finish, slight variations in the visible wave pattern, surface tone, and texture may naturally occur from knife to knife. These differences are part of the character of layered steel and make each piece subtly unique.

As with all kitchen knives, long-term sharpness and appearance depend on proper care and maintenance. While the titanium coating improves corrosion resistance and surface durability, normal signs of use — including minor scratches or gradual wear from repeated sharpening and daily cooking — may develop over time.

Manufacturing timelines may also be affected by factors outside our direct control, including material availability, international logistics conditions, customs procedures, or production scheduling adjustments. If unexpected delays occur, we will provide transparent updates to all backers throughout the process.

All product images and renderings are intended to represent the final design as accurately as possible. However, slight differences in color tone, surface finish, Damascus pattern visibility, or packaging details may occur between production batches.

Our goal is to deliver a kitchen knife that balances aesthetics, functionality, and long-term everyday usability. We are committed to maintaining careful quality control throughout production and fulfillment to ensure every MURASAME ZEN meets the standards presented in this campaign.
