Risks and challenges
L3TRONIX is not an idea on paper. L3OS and L3SYNC work today, on real hardware, with 135 active platforms. That eliminates the biggest risk of any crowdfunding campaign: that the product doesn't exist.

But it would be dishonest to say there are no challenges ahead. There are, and we name them straight.

L3STORE and L3NET require serious infrastructure.

Both are the most ambitious pieces of the ecosystem. L3STORE needs a stable backend, CDN and a publishing system accessible to independent developers. L3NET requires its own servers across multiple regions to guarantee acceptable latency for real netplay. None of this is trivial. The plan is to go region by region, starting with Europe, and scale only when the foundation is solid.

The team is small.

The project is driven by one person, with planned external support for specific phases of L3STORE and L3NET. That's a strength in terms of speed and control, but it means timelines depend on a single line of development. We manage this with conservative planning: roadmap deadlines have real margin built in, not optimistic dates.

Cloud infrastructure prices are rising.

VPS and server costs in Europe have risen significantly in 2026. The campaign budget includes a specific contingency buffer to absorb these variations without compromising development.

What if the goal isn't reached?

Two years of development. Working software.. This is not a promise — it's accumulated work that exists and that anyone can see. This campaign doesn't fund an idea: it funds the next stage of something that is already alive. The only real risk is that this work gets left halfway through for lack of resources. That's why we're here. That's why it matters that this reaches the finish line
