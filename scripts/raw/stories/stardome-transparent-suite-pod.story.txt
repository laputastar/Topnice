I've always loved camping.
For me, the best part was never the destination. It was the night. Lying still, watching the sky fill with stars, feeling that rare kind of quiet that's almost impossible to find anywhere else. That feeling — peaceful, present, completely removed from everything — is what I kept coming back for.
But as the years went on, I couldn't ignore the trade-offs.
Pitching tents under the blazing sun. Cold ground beneath an air mattress. Two tents for one family, because one was never enough — and lying awake at night wondering if everyone in the other tent was warm, comfortable, safe. By the time we'd set everything up, the night sky we'd come for had already slipped past.
I kept thinking: why does this have to be the choice?
Why does being close to nature mean giving up comfort? Why does sleeping under the stars mean lying on the ground in the cold, separated from the people you came with? Why hasn't anyone built a space that gives you both — immersed in the outdoors, protected by the indoors, the whole family together, the whole sky above you?
That question became StarDome.
A space where you can lie in a real bed, warm and sheltered, and watch the stars move across the sky above you. Where your whole family gathers under one transparent ceiling instead of split across two tents. Where the sky is never something you have to choose between and your comfort.
StarDome exists because that choice should never have been necessary in the first place.
