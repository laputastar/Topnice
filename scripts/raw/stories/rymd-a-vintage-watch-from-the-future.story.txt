Risks and challenges
I have run three Kickstarter campaigns before this one. Okapi in 2018. Artefact in 2024. Maestro in 2025. All delivered.

Rymd is produced by the same factory partner that delivered Triomphe and Maestro. They have produced watches for established brands at much larger volumes for over 20 years. The prototype photographs on this page are theirs. The production process has been tested across three previous campaigns.

My approach is to plan conservatively and communicate openly. The Rymd timeline is structured around a February 2027 shipping window with a buffer month built in. If anything affects the timeline, I report it clearly and early. That is the same standard I held to in Okapi, Artefact, and Maestro.

Quality control happens twice. On site at the factory before parcels leave, then a second pass in Stockholm before they go out to backers. Shipping is via tracked international courier, direct to the backer.

Backers are responsible for any duties, VAT, or GST applied by their own country. These vary by jurisdiction and Pedral Design Studio AB cannot pay or compensate for them. Where possible, the amounts are calculated and added during the post campaign pledge manager so the delivery experience stays smooth.

I am not a customer service desk pretending to be the founder. I am the founder. If something goes wrong, I take responsibility. In past campaigns that has meant covering repairs, working with a watchmaker local to the backer, or sending a replacement watch. I cover shipping in both directions. I stay in contact through the whole process.

Three campaigns delivered. Zero broken promises. Rymd will be the fourth.
