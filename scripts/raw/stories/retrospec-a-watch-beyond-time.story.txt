Risks and challenges
Production and Manufacturing
While we have established relationships with experienced manufacturing partners, unexpected delays may occur due to material sourcing, production scheduling, or quality control processes.
To mitigate this, we are working with reliable suppliers and have already validated key components in advance.

Quality Control
Maintaining consistent quality is a top priority. As production scales, there is always a risk of variation.
We address this by implementing strict inspection standards and conducting multiple quality checks before shipment.

Logistics and Shipping
Global shipping conditions may cause delays, especially due to customs procedures or international logistics disruptions.
We will work with experienced logistics partners and keep backers informed of any changes.

Timeline Risks
Although we have planned a realistic production schedule, unforeseen circumstances may impact delivery timelines.
We are committed to transparency and will provide regular updates throughout the process.

High-End Specifications, Made Possible In-House

Founded in 1967 in Azumino, Nagano

“SEIKO” 「精工」means “precision craftsmanship” in Japanese and is not related to any specific brand.

NAN AN SEIKO has built its expertise through precision machinery, parts manufacturing, and watch-related assembly.

Today, the company bringsdecades of manufacturing know-how into its own handcrafted watches, combining Japanese precision engineering with timeless design.

Zaratsu Polishing

A Traditional Japanese Technique for a Flawless Mirror Finish

Zaratsu polishing is a highly skilled Japanese finishing technique used tocreate a perfectly flat,distortion-free surface.

By polishing the frame with exceptional precision, it enhances both the beauty and functionality of the watch design. Outside of major watch manufacturers,only a very small number of craftsmen in Japanare said to master this advanced technique.

Exceptional Legibility

A Larger-Looking Dial, Classical Hands, and Light-Catching Indexes

Retrospec’s dial is designed to look larger and read more clearly by expanding the visible dial opening. The classical combination ofsasaken-style handsandprecise minute markersmakes the time easy to read at a glance.

Ananti-reflective coatingis applied to the inner surface of the glass, helping absorb glare and reflections. This makes the dial even easier to read in various lighting conditions.

Designed to Catch the Light

Each raised index is carefully chamfered to reflect light.

Raised bar indexes arecarefully chamfered to reflect lightat the right angles, enhancing both visibility and the refined brilliance expected from a dress watch.

Luminous paint, a Japanese invention, is also applied to the dial and hands,improving readability even in the dark.

Mechanical Watch Movement

No Battery, Slim Design, Everyday Usability

Retrospec is powered by a mechanical movement, requiringno battery.Designed with a focus onslimness, comfort, and refined aesthetics, it offers the charm of traditional watchmaking in a form that is easy to wear every day.

Skeleton Caseback

A Window into the Mechanical Movement

The skeleton caseback reveals theintricate gears of the mechanical movement,allowing you to enjoy the beauty and craftsmanship inside the watch.

Blued Steel Screws

A Traditional Watchmaking Detail, Visible Through the Caseback
