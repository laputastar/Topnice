Risks and challenges
AutoFILL has already progressed well beyond the concept stage. Working prototypes have been built and tested with beta users, allowing us to refine the design, firmware and assembly process. However, as with any new hardware product, we still face challenges that we continue to manage. We need your help to get it to the next stage. 

The system uses widely available electronic components and standard fittings to reduce supply chain risks. If a specific component becomes difficult to source, compatible alternatives can be substituted without major redesign.

Firmware development is ongoing to ensure reliable pump control, accurate sensor readings and safe operation. This work continues alongside beta testing so we can refine the system before full production.

The kit version requires assembly, so we are preparing clear step-by-step instructions and a full video build guide. The design uses simple mechanical parts and pre-made wiring harnesses to keep the process straightforward.

Production and fulfilment will be handled by the Monocure3D team in Sydney. With more than 45 years of experience in manufacturing and shipping products worldwide, we are well prepared to manage production and delivery.

Environmental commitments

Resin 3D printing often comes down to managing a few key challenges around the print itself.

Running out of resin mid-print, handling messy manual refills, constantly checking on long jobs, and resin separation during extended prints can all lead to failed prints, wasted time & money.

These are common issues that interrupt workflow and make reliable printing harder than it needs to be.

It’s time to remove the guesswork around resin levels and keep every print consistent from start to finish.

Set it up. Press print. Walk away.

AutoFILL Solves the Problems

AutoFILL is designed to address these issues directly, with features that work in real printing environments.

Every resin printer relies on a stable vat level to produce consistent results.

When levels drop during a print, it can lead to failed layers, incomplete parts, and wasted time. AutoFILL automatically maintains the correct resin level throughout the entire print, eliminating the need for manual top-ups and reducing the risk of failure.

The system monitors the resin level in real time and responds as needed. It adds resin when levels drop, and can also empty or circulate the resin to maintain consistency during long prints or idle periods.

Once installed, AutoFILL becomes part of your normal workflow rather than something you need to think about.

AutoFILL is quick to set up and simple to use.

Just place the UV-safe silicone tube in any-size bottle or container, select the desired automatic mode, and position the sensor. The system runs in the background, maintaining consistent resin levels throughout your print, emptying the vat when needed, or cycling the resin to prevent settling during idle periods such as weekends.

Controlled using a simple rotary dial and colour display.

The menu system is designed to be quick to navigate, even when wearing gloves. From here, you can configure automatic filling, set mixing cycles, empty the vat, adjust safety timers and customise how the system operates with your printer.

The menu is organised into clear, easy-to-follow sections.

Navigate between modes, settings, and information, with a simple structure that makes everything fast to find. Rotate the dial to navigate the menu icons, then press to select the desired function.

AutoFILL is built around practical features designed to solve everyday printing challenges.

Each function works together to maintain stable resin levels, reduce handling, and deliver consistent results across every print.

Built with core features designed by people who actually 3D print.

AutoFILL is designed for day-to-day use, automatically managing resin levels, reducing manual handling, and keeping prints consistent from start to finish. It helps prevent failures, minimises mess, and keeps your workflow running without interruption.
