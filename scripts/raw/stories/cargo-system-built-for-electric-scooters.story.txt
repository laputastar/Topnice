Risks and Challenges

Like every hardware project, bringing the RADE Cargo Rack Assembly to market comes with challenges. Fortunately, we have intentionally structured this project to minimize risk wherever possible.

- Product Development

The core engineering work has already been completed for the initial launch platforms. Functional prototypes have been designed, tested, and refined using CAD, 3D printing, CNC machined parts from PCBWAY and real-world fitment verification.  There are a lot of scooters at the office right now.

While final production refinements may continue, we are not starting from a concept sketch or idea. The project has progressed well beyond the early design stage.

- Manufacturing

The RADE CRA is designed around straightforward manufacturing processes including:

* CNC-machined components from a supplier we have known for 20 years, in fact once we reach funded level, everything begins thanks to this relationship, we don't have to wait until closing day.
* Carbon fiber tubes are sourced
* Commercially available hardware (metric bolts)
* Flat-pack cargo basket construction

No complex tooling and injection molds for production, we reduce both cost and manufacturing risk.

- Supply Chain

Where possible, RADE utilizes established suppliers and commercially available materials rather than custom-made proprietary components.

This allows us to:

* Maintain multiple sourcing options
* Reduce lead-time exposure
* Improve production flexibility
* Scale manufacturing more efficiently

- Shipping

One of the largest challenges in the cargo accessory industry is shipping oversized products.

RADE baskets are intentionally designed as flat-pack bolt-together assemblies, dramatically reducing package size and shipping costs while also reducing the likelihood of shipping damage.

This approach helps control costs from manufacturer to RADE and from RADE to customers.

- Platform Expansion

Electric scooter manufacturers regularly introduce new models and revisions.

Our initial launch focuses on platforms that have already demonstrated strong market adoption. Additional scooter models will be added as fitment validation is completed.  If we don't support your make/model head over to our website and get a campaign going for us to do so.  Just find twenty friends to sign up and we will order the scooter.

- Delivery Schedule

As with any physical product, delays can occur due to:

* Supplier lead times
* Material availability
* Shipping disruptions
* Production scheduling
* Bizarre behavior by governments, usually our government in particular

We have intentionally chosen manufacturing methods and suppliers that minimize these risks, but we believe transparency is important and unexpected delays can occur.

- Our Commitment

Our goal is simple:

Deliver a premium cargo system that we proudly use ourselves.

We would rather delay shipment briefly to ensure quality than rush a product that does not meet our standards.

Backers will receive regular updates throughout the manufacturing and fulfillment process, and we will remain transparent about our progress every step of the way.

The RADE Cargo Rack Assembly (CRA)

Electric scooters are great transportation.  They're fast, affordable, nimble, efficient, and surprisingly capable.  But they're not veryuseful,beyond getting you and whatever you can carry in a backpack from point A to B.

The moment you need to carry groceries, pick up takeout, bring tools to work or haul everyday essentials, the scooter often stops being the best transportation choice.   Because it can't carry what you need.

For years, cargo solutions for electric scooters have been an afterthought. Generic baskets, flimsy handlebar bags, and improvised attachments that compromise stability or worse, steering.  Most simply aren't built for daily use.  Or just a big back pack.  We believed e-scooters deserved something better.

So we started with a simple question:

What would happen if scooter cargo systems were designed to unlock a useful in town transportation vehicle?

The result is the RADE Cargo Rack Assembly (CRA).
