HOW GORVIK IS MADE

Damascus steel starts with two different types of steel rod — in this camping axe,1095HC and 15N20.The rods are stacked in alternating layers and heated in a forge to welding temperature. Then they're hammered together, folded, stretched and hammered again over and over.

That process produces200+ layers.The steel is then twisted during forging — this is what gives the finished blade its flowing, wood-grain-like pattern. When the blade is etched in acid, the two steels respond differently: the 15N20 brightens, the 1095 darkens, and the pattern appears.

No two axes come out the same. The pattern you receive is the one that was forged into that specific axe.

This is where this camping axe stands apart from everything else on the market. There are no factories involved. No assembly lines. No machines pressing out identical copies. Every camping axe is built entirely by hand from the very first step to the very last.

1095 High Carbon Steel + 15N20.These two steels are the classic Damascus pairing for a reason.

1095HC holds an edge hard. 15N20 adds nickel to the mix, which gives the pattern its contrast when etched and brings toughness to the blade. Together they create a steel that performs well, holds an edge and looks unlike anything you can buy off a shelf.200+ layers.Twisted pattern. Every axe is its own piece.
