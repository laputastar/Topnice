About the factory

Hi everyone,I'm representing my company (factory)as the creator of this project. I'm not a well‑known designer, but there's one thing worth sharing: I used to work part‑time as a safety instructor.

In those years, I witnessed far too many traffic accidents — some from speeding, some from carelessness — but at the heart of almost every one was a lapse in safety awareness. Those images never left me.

So I asked myself: could I make something that, quietly and without a lot of noise, would remind me — and the people around me —to put safety first?

That thought was the starting point of this watch.To offer the best value for money, I will not run ads. I will shoot the video myself. There will be no watch box and no printed manual. The money saved will be used to lower the crowdfunding price. I will prepare 50 units in stock first. Super Early Bird backers will receive priority shipping. Others will have to wait about two~three months after the campaign ends. Please help spread the word. Thank you!

Limited-time private customization

The hands and markers of the watch are coated with Swiss high-intensity luminous material — a non-radioactive photoluminescent pigment that works like a rechargeable light battery. It absorbs sunlight or artificial light and then glows for hours in the dark, without needing any battery or external light source. Whether you're at night or camping in a tent, you can always read the time clearly.

This watch fits comfortably on both larger and smaller wrists.

Design Inspiration From Hub to Wrist

I love the mechanical aesthetics of cars, especially the wheel hub — those radiating metal spokes that carry speed while holding the direction. So I thought, why not bring that onto a watch dial?

That's how the design came to life: five sets of evenly spaced spokes spreading outward, just like a car wheel hub. The red brake ring on the dial was a deliberate choice — it's not a decoration, it's a signal. Red means stop. Red means brake. Every time you glance at your wrist, I hope that touch of red will remind you:

Speed can be fast, but put safety first.
