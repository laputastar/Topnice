Risks and challenges
Golfeye combines optical hardware, on-device computer-vision helpers, and a 3-axis stabilized gimbal. We've completed multiple prototype rounds, validated core functions on real golf courses, and produced a 200-unit pilot batch to de-risk production. Below are the main risks we've identified and how we plan to mitigate them.

HARDWARE & MANUFACTURING. Minor last-mile surprises are normal for any hardware project. Our 3-4 month delivery window includes buffer to absorb minor delays, and the 200-unit pilot batch has already given us real production-line learnings before mass production starts.

SUPPLY CHAIN. Some components — the Sony image sensor and Qualcomm processor — have global lead-time variability. We've pre-allocated key components with signed quotes and a 60-day inventory buffer, with backup suppliers identified for non-critical parts. If a critical component faces a true shortage, we will communicate immediately and offer backers a clear refund or wait option.

INTERNATIONAL LOGISTICS. Shipping to Canada, the UK, and the EU involves customs and freight steps outside our direct control. We work with an established 3PL partner serving these regions, and we pay all customs and import duties for CA / UK / EU backers — no surprise charges at delivery.

SOFTWARE & FIRMWARE. All core capture functions ship working at launch — auto-detection, swing capture, ball tracking, gimbal control, and app review. Software refinements and any expanded on-device model capabilities will roll out post-delivery via free firmware updates. We will publish a firmware roadmap so backers know what ships at launch vs. what arrives later.

COMMUNICATION COMMITMENT. Hardware projects fail more often from poor communication than poor execution. We commit to transparent monthly Kickstarter Updates throughout production and fulfillment — through good news, bad news, or "still grinding through this part." We won't be that project.

Capture the Shot You'll Talk About Forever

Golfeye G20 is the AI golf camera built to film your shot on a real golf course — not just analyze it indoors.

Most great shots disappear before anyone gets to see them. The swing happens fast, the reaction happens once, and a phone rarely captures the full moment from impact to landing. Golfeye changes that.

Set it up on the tee box with the included ground spike. Aim it toward the target. The system handles the rest — capturing your swing, following the ball over distance, and zooming into the landing point. All automatically.

Whether it's a routine round, a coaching session, or that once-in-a-lifetime hole-in-one — Golfeye gives youproof of the moment, not just the memory.

How Golfeye Works

Set it up. Take your shot. Get the moment.The whole automatic capture takes about10 seconds end to end.

Place Golfeye on the tee box using the included ground spike.

Press Start to activate the system.

Golfeye's AI scans the green and locates the flag, then locks onto the ball at the tee.

The voice prompt confirms: "Please hit the ball."

As you swing, Golfeye captures the moment with its variable optical zoom — the AI adjusts the focal length in real time to keep your swing, the ball, and the landing in frame.

The 3-axis stabilized gimbal pans with the ball flight, tracking it across the fairway.

The 18× zoom locks onto the landing point and captures the impact.

Golfeye sweeps back along the ball path and records your reaction for ~10 seconds.
