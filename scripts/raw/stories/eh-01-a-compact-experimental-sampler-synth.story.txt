Risks and challenges
EH-01 has working prototypes, tested firmware and a planned production process, but this is still the first production run of a hardware product. The main risks are parts delays, small-batch case and knob production, scaling the build process, quality control, firmware refinement, and international shipping delays.

The production plan is based around batch assembly. Electronics will be assembled, programmed and tested in groups, while enclosures and knobs will be produced using small-batch moulding processes. If the campaign reaches higher volumes, some repeated parts such as knobs, and potentially keycaps, may move to injection moulding to improve consistency and speed up production.

There may be small cosmetic variations between units, especially in cast or small-batch parts. These variations will not affect the function of the instrument, and parts that do not meet the required standard will be rejected during quality control.

Small-batch production may result in minor cosmetic variation between units. Final colours, textures, knobs, keycaps, buttons and printed details may differ slightly from prototype photography, but these changes will not alter the core control layout or function of the instrument.

Firmware is already working on the prototypes, but small refinements, bug fixes and improvements may continue during production. EH-01 is designed to be firmware-updatable over USB, so updates can also be released after fulfilment if needed.

Shipping will be charged after the campaign through Kickstarter Pledge Manager. International shipping from Australia can be affected by postage costs, customs processing and delays outside my control. I will provide estimated shipping ranges before launch and keep backers updated if anything changes.

The goal is to keep the project realistic, focused and transparent. If delays or problems come up, I will communicate them clearly through project updates rather than pretending everything is fine while the workshop quietly catches fire.

Multiple synth systems, SD + memory playback, glitch, loop, morph, record and repeat in a small hands-on instrument built for immediate noise, rhythm and strange little moments.

Built for strange samples, rough textures, glitchy rhythms, vocal tones, noise, loops and fast sound-making. It is designed to be played directly: turn a knob, hit a key, break the sound, record it, move on.

This campaign will fund the first small production run of EH-01.

EH-01 combines a compact sampler, digital synth engine and hands-on effects system in a small desktop instrument. It includes built-in memory sample banks/drum machine, SD sample playback, USB MIDI/audio, internal recording, VOX vocals, waveform shaping, filtering, bitcrushing, delay, reverb, chorus and direct control over the sound.

The goal is immediacy. EH-01 is not built around a screen or menu system. The controls are exposed on the front panel so the instrument can be played, pushed and abused without stopping to scroll through settings.

EH-01 is a throwback to an era before every object needed a screen.

Pocket calculators, cassette futurism, wired headphones, compact communicators, military controls and slightly overbuilt pieces of equipment designed to do one thing well.

Its visual language sits somewhere between punk electronics and low-budget NASA hardware: practical, strange, direct and a little optimistic about the future.

EH-01 is not trying to disappear into an app. It is a physical instrument with exposed controls, visible structure and a clear sense of purpose, something you pick up, plug in and use.

EH-01 grew out of years of small synth experiments, prototype boards, sample engines, handmade enclosures and late-night “what happens if this knob does that?” moments.

I wanted a compact instrument that felt direct and physical, something closer to an object you play than a device you operate. No hidden pages, no touchscreen, no carefully polished preset machine. Just a small box of samples, synthesis, controls, low-latency response and a large audio range.

The result is EH-01: a small experimental synth/sampler designed for quick ideas, odd sounds, rough rhythms, quickly recordable and playable noise.

At the heart of the instrument is a Teensy 4.1, a high-speed 32-bit ARM Cortex-M7 processor running at hundreds of megahertz, paired with a dedicated stereo audio codec for true 44.1 kHz digital audio output.
