Risks and challenges
WasteWorks combines art production, AI processing, robotic assembly, material sourcing, and large-format shipping. Each of these steps includes challenges.

Material variability

Waste materials are inconsistent in color, thickness, texture, and durability. This is part of the beauty of the project, but it also means each artwork requires careful selection and testing.

Robotic precision

The robotic process may require calibration depending on the fragment type, size, and placement strategy. We have already developed a prototype, but scaling the process to multiple commissioned artworks requires refinement.

Custom portrait complexity

Some portraits may require more tonal detail than others. We will work with backers to select images that translate well into the WasteWorks material language.

Large artwork shipping

A 140 × 140 cm artwork requires careful packaging and shipping. We will develop protective crating and work with experienced art shipping providers where needed.

Production time

Because each artwork is unique and material-based, production cannot be rushed like a printed object. Our timeline includes room for testing, review, and finishing.

WasteWorks: AI + Robotics Turning Waste into Art

Every day, we throw away materials that still have color, texture, memory, and value.

Paper scraps. Textile leftovers. Cardboard. Packaging. Fragments from everyday life.

Most of these materials disappear into bins, recycling streams, or landfills.WasteWorks brings them back into view.

We transform discarded waste fragments into large-scale portrait artworks using a combination ofartistic composition, image analysis, and robotic-assisted assembly.

From a distance, you see a face.Up close, you see the waste.

Each portrait is made from real physical fragments, arranged like pixels across a large canvas. The result is not a print, not a simulation, and not a digital image. It is a tactile artwork made from materials that were once considered disposable.

We have already built and tested our first working prototype. Now we are launching this Kickstarter to refine the process, improve the robotic workflow, and produce the first limited series of140 cm × 140 cm WasteWorks portraits.

What Is WasteWorks?

WasteWorks is a new art-making process that turns discarded materials into portrait artworks.

A portrait image is first translated into a simplified composition of tones, colors, and material zones. We then sort and prepare waste fragments — such as paper, textile scraps, cardboard, and packaging — according to their visual qualities.

The robotic system helps place these fragments onto a canvas, one piece at a time, following the composition logic of the portrait.

The final artwork exists between image and material.

It is a portrait.It is a mosaic.It is a record of what we throw away.

Why We Created It

Waste is usually hidden.

We put it in a bin, send it away, and forget about it. But waste is also a record of how we live: what we consume, what we value, and what we discard.

WasteWorks asks a simple question:

Can waste become something people want to look at, keep, collect, and talk about?

Instead of treating sustainability only as a technical problem, we want to make it visible and emotional. We believe art can help people see waste differently — not only as an environmental burden, but as a material with cultural and creative potential.

What We Have Built So Far

We have already produced a working prototype that demonstrates the core process:

1. Portrait selectionA portrait or image is selected as the basis for the artwork.

2. Image analysisThe image is translated into areas of light, dark, tone, color, and texture.

3. Material sortingWaste fragments are collected and organized according to their visual properties.

4. Robotic-assisted placementA robotic system helps arrange the fragments as physical pixels on the canvas.

5. Final artworkThe result is a large-scale portrait made from reclaimed materials.

This prototype proves the core idea: discarded fragments can be transformed into a recognizable, powerful image through a combination of design, technology, and material reuse.
