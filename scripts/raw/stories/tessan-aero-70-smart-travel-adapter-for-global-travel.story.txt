Risks and challenges
At TESSAN, we have a proven track record of designing innovative power and charging accessories. Our relentless pursuit of excellence in this category recently earned our gear the title of "Best Travel Adapter" in Esquire’s 2026 Travel Awards, alongside top recognitions from authoritative tech outlets like Pocket-lint. With our mature supply chain and rigorous engineering standards, we are well-prepared to bring the TESSAN AERO 70 to life.
However, pushing the boundaries of hardware innovation inherently comes with unique engineering hurdles. We believe in 100% transparency with our backers regarding our design choices:
1. The "Ultra-Thin" Trade-Off: A Tailored 2-Pin AC Outlet
To create the world's most travel-friendly, pocket-sized smart adapter, we set an uncompromising constraint on the AERO 70's thickness. Incorporating a traditional, deep Universal AC receptacle (which needs to accept large UK 3-pin or AUS angled plugs) physically contradicts this razor-thin profile. Therefore, we made a precise engineering compromise: instead of a bulky universal front, we retained a streamlined 2-pin AC socket strictly limited to US and EU specifications. This ensures you still have AC utility for essentials like shavers or camera chargers from these regions, while keeping the AERO 70 exceptionally slim. For everything else, the multi-port high-speed USB hub has you covered.
2. Hitting the Edge of Physics: Maximum Power Output Limit
Packing advanced circuitry, a real-time smart digital display, and high-efficiency power distribution into such a hyper-compact shell pushes thermal management to its absolute limits. Given the current laws of physics and thermodynamics, the wattage configured in the AERO 70 is the absolute maximum safe power output achievable in this exact volume. It delivers phenomenal power for your mobile setup, but increasing the wattage further in this same ultra-thin form factor is scientifically not feasible in the short term.
3. Supply Chain and Global Logistics
While our global manufacturing lines are primed and our historical delivery record speaks for itself, global shipping still has macro-variables. Unexpected factors—such as material delays or customs congestion—could slightly shift our timeline. We have built buffer times into our estimated delivery schedules and have contingency plans in place.
Our Promise to You:
Should we face any bumps along the way, we won’t stay quiet. We are committed to an open channel of communication through consistent Kickstarter Updates until every AERO 70 safely powers your next journey.
