[RISKS & CHALLENGES]
Risks and challenges
Like any hardware project, BitCards involves several challenges including manufacturing timelines, supply chain availability, and logistics.

The core components of BitCards are printed circuit boards and electronic components, which means production depends on component availability and factory scheduling. To reduce this risk, the system has been designed using widely available electronic components and standard PCB manufacturing processes. We are also working with experienced electronics manufacturers to ensure consistent production quality.

Another challenge is scaling fulfillment if the campaign exceeds expectations. To address this, we are planning fulfillment with experienced logistics partners and scalable shipping solutions.

Software development for the BitCards learning platform is another important part of the project. The platform is already under active development and will continue to evolve alongside the hardware.

Finally, as with any international shipping project, there may be delays related to customs or transportation. We will communicate transparently with backers and provide regular updates throughout the process.

Our goal is to deliver a reliable and well-designed educational product, and we are committed to working through any challenges to ensure backers receive their rewards.

What Is BitCards?

BitCards is a hands-on STEM learning system where kids learn digital logic, sensors, and microcontrollers through story-driven missions.

Instead of starting with code, BitCards starts with the foundation behind every computer, robot, game console, and smart device:

AND. OR. NOT. XOR. Memory. Signals. Inputs. Outputs.

Each BitCards logic card represents a real digital logic concept. Kids connect the cards, test the result, and use them to solve missions inside the BitCards platform.

Every mission moves the story forward.

One mission may ask them to restore the ship’s door control system.Another may require them to activate emergency lights.Another may challenge them to repair the launch sequence before time runs out.

They are not just learning electronics.

They are saving the ship.
