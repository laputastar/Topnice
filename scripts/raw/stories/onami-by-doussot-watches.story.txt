[RISKS & CHALLENGES]
Risks and challenges
ONAMI is not a concept. It is a watch in active 
production with tooling already paid in full.

As an industrial designer, I have learned that 
prototyping is not optional — it is mandatory. I would 
never launch a product without physical samples in hand. 
That is why four prototypes have been produced using the 
actual production tooling — one for each dial variant. 
Not renders. Not approximations. Real watches, built with the same tools that will produce every ONAMI in this production run.

Before asking a single backer for a dollar, a significant 
personal investment has already gone into ONAMI — 
industrial design, engineering, tooling, and four 
production-quality prototypes. The financial commitment 
is real and already made. This campaign goal covers the 
production deposit only — nothing else.

If any tweaks are needed after prototype review, they 
will be minimal and straightforward to implement. 
The hard work is already done.

TIMELINE RISK
Watch manufacturing involves many components and 
dependencies. Delays can occur — at the factory, in 
shipping, or at customs. We have built buffer into the 
November 2026 delivery estimate to absorb minor delays. 
If a significant delay occurs, backers will be notified 
immediately with a revised timeline.

PRODUCTION RISK
All components are A-grade CNC machined. Prototypes are 
being reviewed in detail before production is approved. 
Any quality issues identified at prototype stage will be 
resolved before the production order is placed.

FUNDING RISK
This is an all-or-nothing campaign. If the goal is not 
reached, no charges are made. The CA$55,000 campaign goal 
covers the production deposit only — enough to place the 
order and begin manufacturing. At 154 watches — just over 
half the 300-unit production run — production costs are 
fully covered.

Laurent Doussot  personally performs final regulation, 
QC, strap installation, and packaging on every watch 
before it ships. There is no middleman between the 
designer and the backer.
Learn about accountability on Kickstarter

ONAMI was designed to do both —A sculptural dive watch built to A-grade standard, conceived in Montréal, a 300-unit production run, with Founder Edition pieces individually serialed and engraved.

WHO IS LAURENT DOUSSOT

Some stories begin in a workshop. Mine began in the ocean.

Laurent Doussot is a Montréal-based industrial designer, surfer, sailor, diver — and internationally award-winning knifemaker. For over two decades, he shaped steel into custom folding knives: objects where precision, durability, and artistry had to coexist. Objects made to be used, not just admired. That discipline taught him that small details matter, that textures tell stories, and that the way something feels in the hand is as important as how it performs.

When he turned to watchmaking, he knew exactly what he did not want to create. Not another homage. Not a simple tool. A watch that carried the spirit of the ocean — a lifelong passion — and the precision of twenty years shaping steel by hand.That vision became ONAMI.

ONAMI — 大波 — Big Wave

The force of open water. Powerful, inevitable, beautiful. Built for the water — refined for everywhere else.

316L forged stainless steel • 3-piece construction 43mm • 49mm lug-to-lug • 13mm thick • 22mm lug width Brushed planes • polished edges • stamped lug texture.Every line has a reason. Every finish has an intention.Except for the movement, every component of ONAMI is custom designed — the case, the crowns, the hands, the dial — all produced exclusively for this watch.Everything you see and touch was made specifically for ONAMI.

THE FEATURE THAT DEFINES IT

Internal rotating bezel — operated by the screw-down crown at 9 o'clock. A deliberate nod to the legendary super-compressor design.

43mm diameter. 49mm lug-to-lug. Substantial presence without bulk. The curved caseback sits naturally against the wrist — the three-piece forged case was shaped to wear, not just to be seen.
