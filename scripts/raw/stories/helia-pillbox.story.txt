Fixing the Old Problem

Millions of people take medication daily- andwe're all familiar with clunky plastic pill cases. These medicine organizers are challenging to open, and designed to be out of sight - in a drawer, or buried in a bag. We canfeelwhy;They shout "clinical" at the top of their lungs.If you take medication -at home or out for the day- you deserve something that fits your life.

I prototyped the first Helia a decade ago, to help a friend feel more at ease traveling with her daily medication. For her - and I hope you too - it made the shift from medical necessity to a personal accessory.

It transforms 'clinical medicating' into a ritual: 'caring for you.'

The Story Behind Helia

Over ten years ago, a close friend was managing daily medication for an invisible illness.

Of course,the only organizer she could findwas the hard plastic box from the pharmacy. She felt stigmas reenforced every time she took it out into the world.

So, I made her a better one.After sketching, planning, and asking questions- I took a block of maple and got started. Using paper templates, Phillips head screws, andlotsof sandpaper, it was ready.

That gift became the first Helia-loved, and used every day. I've made more over the years for family and friends, and eventually myself, as I started needing twice-daily medications. Steadily,  I've honed the design through a decade of prototyping- refining size, shape, function, and feel.

A year ago I was startled to realize that the market need still persists, and decidedit was time to make Helia more broadly available. As an object I love, and have refined for over a decade- I am excited to bring Helia to anyone who wants warmer care than plastic cases can provide.

With your help, it's ready to be yours.

Manufacturing Plan

The Production Plan is ready:

Helia will be made with a trusted manufacturing partner in Southern China. I know this team personally, and they are excited to help bring this project to life with the care it deserves.

Pre-production samples have been validated, the FSC-certified wood source is confirmed, and the machining process is already dialed in.Once the Purchase Order is complete, the team is ready to begin setting up production.

I’ll also have three close colleagues on-site to help oversee the manufacturing ramp-up, quality control, testing, shipping, and logistics. There are always challenges when bringing a physical product into production, but we have the right people in place to work through them anddeliver the first units by the end of 2026."
