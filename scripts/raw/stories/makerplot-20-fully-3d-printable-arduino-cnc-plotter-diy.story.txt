Risks and challenges
Makerplot 2.0 is intentionally minimal, and that simplicity reduces many common hardware risks. However, transparency is important.

Mechanical Precision

Since Makerplot uses no linear rails or bearings, final precision depends on print quality and proper assembly. Clear print settings and calibration instructions will be provided to ensure consistent results.

To address this, the firmware includes software-based backlash compensation, improving positioning accuracy and ensuring clean, consistent drawings.

The entire mechanical system has been designed specifically around these motors to operate within safe torque limits.

Electronics & Supply

All components are standard, widely available, and non-proprietary. Compatible alternatives can be used if needed.

🚀 Makerplot 2.0

The Modular 3D Printable Plotter Without Linear Rails

Faster. Bigger. Smarter. Still radically simple.

Makerplot 2.0 is the evolution of the original Makerplot, redesigned from the ground up to deliver higher speed, greater precision, and unlimited scalability while preserving the philosophy that made it unique:

Extreme mechanical simplicity.

No linear rails

No ball bearings

No metal guide rods

No expensive CNC components

Just 3D printed parts, standard screws, affordable electronics, and your creativity.

🎯 Why Makerplot 2.0?

Most DIY plotters become more complex as they become larger.

Makerplot 2.0 takes the opposite approach.

Its modular architecture allows you to build machines in different sizes using the same design philosophy and the same printable components.

Choose the size that fits your workspace or expand it whenever you want.

Designed to be:

Easy to assemble

Open source friendly

Makerplot 2.0 is more than a plotter.

It is a mechanical platform designed to demonstrate how simplicity can outperform complexity.

⚡ Faster Motion System

Unlike the original version based on geared motors, Makerplot 2.0 uses:

Arduino compatible controller

NEMA17 stepper motors

Standard stepper drivers

This configuration provides:

Better positioning accuracy

Smoother motion

Greater reliability

Compatibility with the maker ecosystem

The result is a machine capable of producing cleaner drawings while significantly reducing plotting time.

🧩 A Completely Modular Design

Makerplot 2.0 is available in multiple printable formats:

A4:Perfect for education, lettering, illustrations and everyday projects.

A3:Ideal for posters, technical drawings and generative art.

A2:Large format plotting for artists, designers and makers.

INFINITE EXPANSION:Thanks to its modular architecture, the system can be extended indefinitely by combining additional structural modules.

Build the machine that fits your ideas instead of adapting your ideas to the machine.

⚙️ No Linear Rails. No Bearings.

Makerplot 2.0 follows a radically different philosophy.

Instead of relying on expensive mechanical components, the entire structure is designed around:

3D printed structural parts

Standard screws

Readily available electronics

This dramatically reduces cost, simplifies assembly and makes replacement parts printable anywhere.

🛠 Easy Assembly

No special tools.

No custom hardware.
