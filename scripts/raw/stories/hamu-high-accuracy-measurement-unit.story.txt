We are a small international team of professional electronic engineers and we were not satisfied with the existing commercial modules with high-resolution ADC.
The goal
of our project is to create a new segment of measurement devices that implement the maximum functionality of ADCs, an efficient design and high-accuracy comparable to reference voltmeters.
The motivation
for creating HAMU was the lack of low-cost technical means on the market for measuring and analyzing signals from highly sensitive detectors, low-noise electronic circuits, and systems with a small output voltage increment, while having a compact design and the ability to reconfigure operating algorithms to create precision electronic devices
(for example, diagnostic systems for the condition of moving parts of high-speed passenger trains, compressors, turbines, aircraft engines; systems for searching for ultra-small leaks and predicting dangerous situations in infrastructure gas networks, analyzing the tightness of spacecraft and vacuum equipment; magnetometric and seismometric equipment for studying rocks, predicting dangerous phenomena during mining operations; hydroacoustic devices for searching for underwater objects or orienting underwater vehicles).
The
concept
of the project is to provide the user with an adaptive tool to solve their technical problems. Firstly, there are no internal amplifiers in HAMU.This is because the use of a specific amplifier type is an individual decision for many projects, and the high-accuracy of HAMU allows measurement of the output signals of some detectors and circuits in direct with a high signal-to-noise ratio. Secondly, HAMU allows measurement the noise spectral density. This is an important function for determining the optimal measurement technique based on the analysis of output signals from sensors and electronic circuits connected to the unit. This is an important function for determining the optimal measurement technique based on the analysis of output signals from sensors and electronic circuits connected to the unit. Thirdly, HAMU has two data input/output ports (USB and UART). The selection of the input signals conversion algorithm is carried out using the “mode selection connector”, commands from a personal computer, portable devices, or an external controller at the discretion of the user. The conversion mode parameters (each algorithm has default parameters) are set using specific commands from external devices, as is the subsequent data output. Fourthly, the creation of a personal measurement method and an algorithm for converting data into digital form will be available to all HAMU users in the future.
