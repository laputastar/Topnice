[RISKS & CHALLENGES]
Risks and challenges
Every hardware campaign carries the same handful of risks. Here they are, plainly — and why each one is unusually low for us.

Manufacturing & quality. No production run is ever entirely risk-free — but ours is about as de-risked as hardware gets: we've already shipped 300,000+ connected devices with the same production and quality-control partners, on proven tooling and materials, not a first-time factory.

Component supply (e-ink & NFC). Component markets can always surprise you, and we won't pretend otherwise — but the display and NFC parts are standard and already sourced, with lead times we've built into our delivery dates.

Shipping & logistics. Cross-border shipping is never perfectly smooth — but we already fulfil to 40+ countries every week and ship from regional hubs, which keeps delivery and customs about as predictable as it gets.

Timeline slips. Dates can always move a little, and we'd rather say so up front — but the product is designed, tested and certified-in-progress, so this campaign funds the production run, not the R&D. If anything shifts, you'll hear it first in a backer update, the way we've talked to our community for ten years.

One honest limit: KEEP is water-resistant — rain and a wet pocket are fine — but it is not built for the washing machine or for immersion, and we will never claim otherwise.

KEEP is a connected keychain that carries a note from the people you love. They write to you in the free app — a few words, a drawing, a sticker — from anywhere in the world. You get a notification, hold your phone to your keychain for a couple of seconds, and their note appears on the little e-ink screen, where it stays until the next one arrives. No battery to charge, no WiFi, no Bluetooth — just a piece of someone who loves you, with you wherever you go.

Every note starts in the free KEEP app — oniPhone and Android. Write a few words, doodle by hand, or drop a sticker, preview it, and send. It lands on your person's KEEP the next time they tap their phone to it.

A notification lands on your phone. Open the KEEP app, hold your phone to the keychain for a second or two, and the new note appears on the e-ink screen — no pairing, no battery, nothing to charge.

Our team spent eight months making KEEPminimalist— and making sure it's high-quality, durable and effortless to use. The hard part wasn't adding features; it was leaving them out.

KEEP isn't a promise from strangers. It's the second hardware product from the team that has been shipping connected wooden Loveboxes since its first Kickstarter in 2017.

Loved in real homes

KEEP wasn't designed in a meeting — it was designed in your inbox. Ten years ago we put love in a wooden box; for a decade you've told us what you wished it could do. KEEP is that wish-list, built: the same heart, made to travel.

For ten years, we've read variations of the same three emails:

"I love my Lovebox. I wish I could carry it with me."— Sophie, Lovebox owner since 2019

"I want one for my grandmother. But she doesn't have WiFi."— Tom, Lovebox owner since 2021

"I send messages to my kids when I travel. I wish they could send me real ones back."— Anna, Lovebox owner since 2018

For ten years, we wrote back: "we hear you, we're working on it." Now, we don't have to anymore.

KEEP didn't come off a spec sheet. It came from eight months of prototypes, dead ends and late nights — and from people in our community who carried early units every day and told us, bluntly, what to fix. By the time it reached this page, it had already lived in real pockets, on real keyrings, on real bags.
