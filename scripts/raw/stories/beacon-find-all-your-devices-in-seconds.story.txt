The Easy Way to Find Any Phone or Tablet

Just press a button to make any nearby phone or tablet ring in seconds - even on silent or do not disturb.  No internet or account setup required.  Works with iOS and Android devices.

Easily Locate Up To 16 Devices

Having 4 buttons doesn't mean you're limited to finding 4 devices.  Holding a button while turning Beacon on sets the group.  With 4 groups of 4 channels, you can easily track down up to 16 devices individually.

Better Than the Alternatives

Beacon is designed specifically for finding nearby phones and tablets.  The existing options are not.Trackers are designed to help you use your phone to find your stuff.  "Find My Device" networks can help in some situations, but they require another device, internet access, more setup, and more time.Beacon is designed to be faster and simpler for finding nearby phones and tablets.

Works Anytime, Anywhere

Beacon uses Bluetooth, soit works anywhere without relying on an internet connection or cell service.  You can take it camping in the middle of nowhere and it will still work.  While you're home, keep it in a drawer, hang it on a hook, or stick it on the fridge so it's always ready when you need it.

Beacon comes with a completely free app that works withboth iOS and Android.  And the best part isthere are no ads, no subscriptions, no accounts, and no data collection.

You can adjust your settings in the app on the fly: group, channel, volume, vibration, and whether the sound plays once or loops until you find your device and hit the stop button.

Cheap Long-Lasting Battery

Most users shouldeasily hit 8-12 yearsbefore the battery runs out.  And when it finally does, it'scheap and easy to replaceit yourself with a readily-available CR2032 coin cell battery.  Check the FAQ if you want to see the math.

Why I Built Beacon

A few years ago, I built the first prototype of Beacon with one simple idea in mind: there's a ton of options for using your phone to find a tracker, but why hasn't anyone made a dedicated device to find your phones and tablets yet?  Admittedly, the first iteration was pretty terrible.  But having a working proof of concept filled me with excitement and I started thinking of all the ways I could make it better.

Before I got around to making any major improvements, I got laid off from my tech job.  After taking some time to reflect, I decided it was the right time to go all in and finish Beacon as an independent project.

Since then I've refined the hardware and software over and over, turning Beacon into something I'm genuinely proud of: an honest product that's both practical and polished.

Why Kickstarter?

Beacon is already built and working, but there are still significant upfront costs involved in bringing it to market.  The biggest one is Bluetooth qualification.  Since Beacon uses Bluetooth, I need to complete the required Bluetooth SIG qualification process before I can legally sell it.  That fee is pretty hefty, and a major hurdle for an independent project like this.

This campaign will help to cover some of that cost, along with the first production run and fulfillment.  Kickstarter is what helps me turn Beacon from a finished project into something I can actually ship.
