[RISKS & CHALLENGES]
Risks and challenges
This is our first Kickstarter campaign, but not our first watch. We have already released multiple collections, sold more than 500 watches and spent the last months developing and testing the modular system behind SCRATCH. We know where the critical points are, and we are going into this campaign with a clear plan. Still, there are some risks:

CD Dial Production
Every SCRATCH dial starts with a real old CD. That means no two pieces are exactly the same. It also means production is more hands-on than with a normal dial. We source the CDs ourselves, choose them one by one, and cut the dials ourselves in Switzerland.
Because of that, there is always the challenge of material handling. CDs can break, peel or scratch if the process is not right. We already spent months testing and refining how to make these dials properly. Still, if a dial does not meet our standard, we remake it. We will not ship pieces we are not proud of.

Modularity and Assembly
SCRATCH is built as a two-module system that we have spent months refining. The modules are designed to switch easily and lock together securely, giving you the freedom to change the look of your watch in seconds
Once production starts, every watch will be assembled and checked carefully. Since this is not a mass product with identical dials, assembly and quality control take more time. We see that as part of the product, not as a problem. It just means we would rather move a little slower than compromise on quality.

Dial Selection After the Campaign
After the campaign, we will contact backers individually so they can choose their preferred dial option. For backers choosing the labeled frontside CD dial, there will be a second step later, where they select their final preferred design from the options available. For the “Your Own CD” option, backers will need to send us their CD after the campaign and mark the area they want us to use.
This process takes more coordination than a standard watch campaign, but it is necessary to make each piece personal and one of one. We will keep this process structured and communicate clearly at every step.

Supply Chain and Timing
Like with every hardware project, delays can happen. Materials, case components, packaging or shipping can take longer than planned. We have built this timeline based on the real steps needed to produce the watches properly, not on an unrealistic best-case scenario.
If something takes longer, we will communicate it openly. We would rather be transparent than make promises we cannot keep.

Fulfillment and Support
Shipping one-of-one watches worldwide also comes with responsibility. Orders need to be packed carefully, assigned correctly and sent out in the right configuration. This matters even more when backers choose different dial options or custom CDs.
We take that seriously. If problems come up, we will deal with them directly and keep backers informed. When you support this project, you are not talking to a faceless company. You are backing a young brand that actually answers.

Our Promise
We are new to Kickstarter, but not new to building watches and not new to working hard for this brand. Provoqué started in a bedroom, grew through real support and got here because people believed in what we do.
If challenges come up, we will face them honestly, communicate clearly and do everything we can to deliver a product worth waiting for.
Learn about accountability on Kickstarter
