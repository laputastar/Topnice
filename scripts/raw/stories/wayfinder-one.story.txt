Build a Full-Scale Digital Steering Wheel for Sea of Thieves and Marine Simulators. Complete 3D Print STL Files & Blueprints

Wayfinder Oneis a full-scale, 36-inch digital ship wheel made for games likeSea of Thievesand sailing simulators. It hooks up to your PC via a cheap Arduino and an optical encoder, turning your real-world steering into in-game movements.

I’m primarily not selling physical wheels that cost a fortune to ship. Instead, this Kickstarter was made for thedigital blueprints, 3D printing STL files, and the codeso you can easily build your own from scratch. The goal was beingAccessible:You just need a standard 3D printer, some basic woodworking tools, and an Arduino. No advanced electronics skills required.

I have already built and coded a fully working prototype that I use to play games right now. I’m raising funds to turn my messy workshop notes, CAD models, and code into a clean, step-by-stepPDF Build Guideand a polished package of STL files that anyone can follow.

I'm a mechanical engineering student and a collegiate athlete. Between classes and sports, I wanted a fun project that let me use my engineering background for something purely awesome. I love video games, and steering a massive ship with a real wheel sounded like a lot of fun. Since nobody made a realistic ship wheel controller, I decided to design one.

I'm just a student-athlete who loves engineering and gaming. I wanted a fun, simple way to make gaming more immersive without spending thousands of dollars, and I want to help other gamers build the same thing.

Risks and challenges

I am still debating on how many actual Wayfinder's I want to build, but it will probably be limited. And as a note, for games like Sea of Thieves, the wheel will never be entirely in sync with the game, as it is impossible to read and interact with the game data.
