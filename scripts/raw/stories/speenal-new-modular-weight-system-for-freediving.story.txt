Risks and challenges
As with any product development, there are potential risks related to production, supply chain, and logistics. However, we have taken several proactive measures to minimize these risks and ensure a smooth delivery process.

Prototyping and testing: We have been working closely with our manufacturing partners for several months. Together, we have already developed and validated multiple prototypes, which allowed us to refine the design and confirm the feasibility of large-scale production.

Committed suppliers: Our suppliers are not new contacts discovered during this campaign. They have been actively involved in the project from the start and are fully aligned with our production plan. Their early involvement greatly reduces the risks linked to industrialization.

Secured supply chain: To avoid delays or shortages, we have implemented a dual-sourcing strategy for critical components. This means that, even in case of disruption with one partner, we can rely on an alternative to keep production on track.

Delivery timeline: We are aware that unexpected challenges can still occur (such as material availability, logistics bottlenecks, or customs delays). To mitigate this, we have built extra time into our schedule. Our priority is to deliver on time, but we will always communicate transparently with our backers if adjustments are needed.

Ongoing industrialization: As we move toward final industrialization, some minor adjustments may still occur in terms of design details, colors, or materials. All images, videos, and 3D renderings shown on this page are non-contractual and may differ slightly from the final product. These refinements are part of our continuous effort to ensure the best quality, comfort, and reliability for the final version.

We believe these measures put us in a strong position to deliver your rewards as planned. Supporting this campaign means joining a project that has been carefully prepared, with realistic goals and robust safeguards in place.

The Problem with Free Diving

For decades, freedivers have been forced to make a frustrating compromise when it comes to weights: having to choose between comfort, versatility, efficiency, and performance.❌The weight belt:affordable, quite versatile, but relatively far from the center of buoyancy for good balance. It can cause lower back pain after long sessions at sea. Furthermore, its adjustment is slow and inconvenient.

❌The neck weight:although better suited to dynamics, it lacks versatility, restricts movement, and causes discomfort and tension in the neck, an area essential for relaxation.

FREE to moveThe strapping has been designed to allow you to perform all kinds of movement. Whether you are hyperextending your arms in front, swimming breaststroke or doing underwater dancing... SPEENAL always fits on your back.

The SPEENAL allows you tocreate numerous combinations, letting you place your weights wherever you want. This gives you ultra-precise balance and neutrality.You can adapt it to your liking depending on your conditions and equipment.Below are someexamples for a 4 kg configuration.

2 weight sizes are available:200g and 300g. With the initial 2 kg weight (empty Speenal = 620g + 1380g of ballast) and the 20 available blister pack slots,you can reach 6 kg with the 200gblisters and8 kg with the 300gblisters.  Of course, you can mix the two sizes.

The dome at the front is shaped like a drop of water so that the water does not create resistance against the equipment. It then slides down the external walls, which are trapezoidal in shape with rigid plates covered with a hydrophobic material.

Ventilation grids are located at the front and rear to allow air to escape, whether you are right-side up or upside down.
