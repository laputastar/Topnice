Two Problems with Firearms Training

1. Front Sight Focus

Most firearms training courses tell students to focus on their front sight so that the target is blurry.

However, in a real-life shooting, you need to be threat focused so that you can process the threat in real-time while also knowing when it is no longer a threat.

2. Scripted Training

Firearms training is mostly scripted.

We tell the student what the drill is, how many shots to fire, and where to shoot. They wait for the buzzer or command to fire, then they complete the drill.

No thinking. No processing. Just theatrics.

But in a real-world gunfight, you don't know how many shots you will need to fire.

I'm Taylor. Marine veteran, former police officer, and mechanical engineer.

I've spent years in the military and law enforcement, watching people train hard with the wrong tools and walk away with a false sense of readiness because of the training problems I listed above.

So I decided to create a solution.

I wanted to create anaffordable anduser-friendlyvisual stimulus shot timer so that students had to stay threat-focused and process information from the threat while shooting.

We say we shoot to stop the threat, but very rarely do we train to stop shooting when the threat stops.

So, one night, after some divine inspiration, I wired the first circuit.

That was the beginning of the Defenders And Disciples Behavioral Optical Decision Device... or.... theDADBODD
