Risks and challenges
Manufacturing
AiScan O1 has completed prototype development, extensive internal testing, and beta validation with external users. Tooling is finalized and manufacturing facilities are ISO-certified with established production lines for precision optical and electronic assemblies. Key component suppliers are contracted with inventory buffers for critical parts (MEMS projectors, AMOLED panels, image sensors).

Risk
Global supply chain disruptions or component shortages could cause delivery delays. Mitigation: We maintain secondary sourcing for all critical components and will communicate timeline changes immediately and transparently.

Regulatory Compliance
AiScan O1 has received FCC certification (FCC ID: 2BTZJ-AiScan O1). CE certification for EU markets is in progress and expected to complete before fulfillment begins. The device complies with IEC 60825-1 laser safety standards for both Class 1 and Class 3R operation modes.

Shipping and Logistics 
Fulfillment will be managed through established logistics partners with distribution centers in the United States, Europe, and Asia. Free shipping is included for US, EU, and UK backers. Shipping to other regions will be available at cost. Import duties and customs fees vary by destination country and are the responsibility of the backer. Full tracking information will be provided for every shipment.

Software and Firmware
The on-device software is production-ready and has been validated through our beta testing program. Post-launch firmware updates will be delivered via Wi-Fi OTA, including performance optimization, new features, and bug fixes informed by community feedback. We are committed to ongoing development of both the on-device software and the planned AiScan Studio desktop application.

3DGS Processing 
The 3DGS pipeline involves edge computing for model reconstruction after on-device capture. We are actively optimizing processing speed and output quality. Network connectivity is required for 3DGS mode. Availability and processing time may vary based on server load during initial launch period.

Every 3D scanner on the market makes the same tradeoff: accuracy or portability. Geometric fidelity or visual realism. Professional workflow or beginner accessibility.

AiScan O1 refuses to choose.

Developed by ChaoXiLi — a 3D vision technology company specializing in high-precision scanning solutions — AiScan O1 is the first handheld 3D scanner to integrateblue-light MEMS structured-light scanningwithon-device 3D Gaussian Splatting. Two fundamentally different capture technologies, unified in a single770g standalone device.

The result: 0.03mm metrological accuracy for engineering applications, and photorealistic 3DGS rendering for visual applications — from the same hardware, in the same session,with no PC required.

An8-core processor, 32GB RAM, and 7-inch 2K AMOLED touchscreenhandle the entire pipeline —scan, edit, mesh, export— on-device. Two built-in AI features eliminate manual parameter configuration and prevent incomplete captures.

This is not an incremental update to existing 3D scanning technology. It is a structural advancement.

3D Gaussian Splatting Why It is and Why It Matters

Conventional 3D scanners reconstruct surfaces astriangulated mesheswith UV-mapped textures. This approach is well-suited for CAD and manufacturing workflows, but fundamentally limited invisual fidelity— mesh rendering cannot accurately represent translucency, sub-surface scattering, anisotropic reflections, or fine volumetric structures like hair and fabric.3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS)is a different representation entirely. Instead of polygon surfaces, 3DGS encodes a scene as a collection of oriented3D Gaussian primitives— each carrying position, covariance, color, and opacity. The result is acontinuous, differentiable representationthat captures how light actually interacts with materials.
