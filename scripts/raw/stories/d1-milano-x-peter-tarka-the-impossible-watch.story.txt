MEET THE COLLECTION

Peter Tarka’s work lives in a world where objects do not have to follow the rules of reality. Forms bend. Surfaces float. Color becomes architecture. His 3D compositions feel playful, precise, and impossible at the same time.

When D1 Milano first discovered Peter’s visual language, we were drawn to a simple question:The first concept was not a traditional watch design. It was a piece of pure 3D imagination: retro-futuristic, sculptural, colourful, and technically impossible in its original form.

So the project became a translation process:

The goal was never to make a normal watch with an artist’s name on it. The goal was to preserve the feeling of Peter’s world and turn it into a real object people could live with every day.

ABOUT THE PRODUCT

Instead of a traditional dial, the Peter Tarka x D1 Milano watch uses three dedicated rotating discs:

Once the time is set, the discs automatically rotate to display the correct time. But the movement does not stop there. Seven small weekday dots gradually turn on as the week progresses. One dot means Monday. Two dots mean Tuesday. By the time all seven dots are lit, it is Sunday.A digital screen duplicates the essential information. So the watch is both expressive and practical: a playful physical display with a clear digital readout when you need it.

BEHIND THE SCENES

The original idea was born in a pure 3D world where gravity, weight, thickness, and manufacturing limits do not matter. But a real watch has to live on a wrist. It has to feel balanced. It has to be readable. It has to be assembled, adjusted, finished, tested, and worn.It was an extensive co-design process between Peter Tarka, the D1 Milano design team and product development team to keep the identity of the concept while making every part physically achievable.

After several months of development, the first prototype sample arrived.

But that was just the beginning …

Peter’s original vision imagined the watch in stainless steel. On screen, that made sense: stainless steel gave the object a strong, solid presence and matched the boldness of the design.

But with such a sculptural case and bracelet, stainless steel made the watch too heavy.So we re-engineered both the case and the bracelet in aluminum.

This allowed us to significantly reduce the overall weight (50% lighter than the steel version), making the watch more comfortable for everyday wear. It also opened up new possibilities for anodised color finishes, allowing the collection to become more expressive and closer to Peter Tarka’s visual universe.Thus, the product found its balance: artistic ambition, engineered for real life. The real product already exists and now, with your support, we would be able to start mass production to bring the collection to backers all around the world.

Every material and proportion was chosen to preserve the bold sculptural identity of Peter Tarka’s design while making the watch lighter, clearer, and more practical for everyday use.

ABOUT PETER TARKA

Peter Tarka is a worldwide known digital design artist, recognised for colourful immersive 3D compositions, impossible forms, and bold geometric worlds.

His work often feels like a place between product design, architecture, sculpture, and visual imagination. Everyday objects become surreal. Shapes behave like structures. Color becomes physical.The Peter Tarka x D1 Milano collection began with his visual thinking: the playful geometry, the impossible proportions, the sense that an object can feel both familiar and completely new.
