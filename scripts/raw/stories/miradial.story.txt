You sit down to work, but somehow the afternoon just slips away. A check here, a scroll there, and nothing gets done. It was never your fault, you just never had the right tool. Until now.
Meet MIRA Dial
Every app, blocker, and productivity device you've tried has the same weakness
—
the off switch is one tap away. MIRA Dial doesn't. You turn the dial to start a session, and every screen you own locks into focus at once. No menu to disable it. No bypass. The session runs until it's done.
Set it once. Then just turn the dial and work.
The Problem
You don't decide to pick up your phone. Your hand's already moving before the thought arrives, and by the time you notice, the time's just gone.
It isn't that you don't care, and it isn't that you're weak. You just never had a tool that could actually stop it. Every app lives on the same device as the distraction, so the way out is always one tap away.
Why Other Blockers fail?
Every blocker you've tried is sitting right there on the same phone you're trying to ignore. You set the rules, but you're also the one who can break them. You're the referee and the player. Of course you bend the rules in your favor.
Screen Time has an "Ignore Limit" button. Freedom app comes off in safe mode. Cold Turkey is a couple of clicks to disable. And the second you lock your phone, the distraction just slides over to your laptop. Blocking one screen was never going to be enough, because there was always a second one waiting.
Can't Quit on Impulse
MIRA Dial isn't software you can switch off the moment the urge hits. Once you press, your devices lock and the easy exit, the one your thumb takes before you've even decided, is gone. There's no "Remind Me in 15 Minutes," no menu, no quick setting to flip mid-session.
The Timer You Race
You can lose a whole afternoon to one annoying task. But tell yourself "I've got twenty minutes," and suddenly it's done in twenty minutes.
It works like running. On your own you jog, but the moment someone's next to you, you're not about to let them win. The timer is that someone. It's right there the whole session, and you just don't want it to beat you. Every break gives you a clean reset, so the next round feels just as short and doable as the first.
Automatic Intervals
Set your cycle once and MIRA Dial handles the rest. When your focus time's up, it changes to break mode and unlocks your distractions for your set minutes as a reward. Then it starts the next round on its own.
You never have to decide when to stop, remember to take a break, or summon the discipline to start again. You just keep moving, and you finish the day sharp instead of fried.
Blocks All Your Devices
Phone, laptop, tablet, browser, all quiet the moment you press. The distraction has nowhere to escape to, because the laptop's already locked, and so is the phone. For once, there's no second screen waiting.
Works across iOS, Android, Mac, Windows, and every major browser simultaneously.
How It Works
1. Set your rules once.
In the app, choose what to block, apps, sites, notifications, across every device you want covered. That's the only setup you'll ever do.
2. Turn the dial.
Set your focus time, your break time, and how many rounds you want to do.
3. Press to lock in.
Every device goes quiet at the same time, and you can finally focus.
The cost
You lose a few hours a day to your phone or laptop. It never feels like much in the moment, just a check here, a scroll there. But it adds up to hundreds, even thousands of hours a year. For anyone whose time is worth money, tens of thousands of dollars are quietly gone.
But the money was never the real cost. It's the dinners you sat through but weren't at. The hobby still waiting. The version of you that keeps not showing up. Win back even an hour a day, and that's what you're actually buying back.
For ADHD Brain
You already know willpower advice is useless. You understand dopamine loops and attention hijacking better than most people, and you still can't get through two focused hours. That's not a knowledge problem. It's a mechanism problem.
Putting your commitment into a physical object is one of the few things that actually works for
attention-diverse brains
, because it takes the decision away from the moment the urge hits. MIRA Dial doesn't ask for your willpower. It just makes the easy escape disappear before the impulse can use it.
No Subscription
Freedom app costs $40 a year. Most focus apps nickel-and-dime you monthly. MIRA Dial has no subscription, no renewal, no features locked behind a paywall. Buy it once, and it's yours.
