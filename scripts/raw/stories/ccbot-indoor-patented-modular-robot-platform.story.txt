Risks and challenges
CCBOT Indoor is a small Founder Batch hardware project, and we are keeping the first run intentionally limited to 10–15 units so we can manage quality, assembly, and support closely.

The main challenge is moving from a working prototype to a small batch of reliable units. Since the robot uses a modular design and 3D-printed body components, small mechanical improvements can be made quickly. If a printed part needs adjustment, we can revise and reprint it instead of relying on expensive tooling.

Some components, such as cameras, ultrasonic sensors, robot base parts, batteries, or electronics, may have minor supply or pricing changes. To reduce this risk, we plan to use widely available components and keep extra parts on hand for testing and replacement.

Each CCBOT Indoor robot will be assembled and tested before shipping. We plan to check basic movement, smartphone control, live camera video support, and ultrasonic / sonar distance sensing before sending units to backers.

CCBOT Indoor is designed as an early developer robot platform for learning, research, indoor inspection, Python development, and ROS-ready experimentation. Some software examples, documentation, and navigation experiments may continue improving after the Founder Batch ships.

Future track-based and water-surface mobility modules are part of the broader CCBOT platform roadmap. They are not included in this first CCBOT Indoor Founder Batch.

Overall, we believe the risks are manageable because this is a small batch, the design is modular, many parts can be replaced or reprinted, and we can work directly with early backers to improve the platform.
