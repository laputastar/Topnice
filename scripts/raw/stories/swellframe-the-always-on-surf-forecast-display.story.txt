[RISKS & CHALLENGES]
Risks and challenges
Swellframe is a hardware product, and hardware always comes with real risks. We want to be upfront about the areas we are watching most closely.

The biggest risk is supply chain timing. Swellframe depends on specialized components, especially the e-ink display, PCB components, batteries, enclosures, and packaging materials. We have already started securing key parts and working with suppliers, but delays can still happen. Component lead times, shipping delays, customs, manufacturing capacity, or quality issues at the supplier level could impact our delivery timeline.

To reduce this risk, we are not starting from zero. We have working prototypes, we have already tested the core experience, and we are actively locking in the most important parts of the supply chain before launch. We are also building in buffer time where possible and working with suppliers that can scale with us.

Another challenge is manufacturing consistency. Swellframe needs to look good, feel simple, and work reliably in real homes. Moving from prototypes to production means validating the enclosure, screen fit, battery performance, firmware stability, and final assembly process. Small changes in materials or tolerances can create unexpected issues, so we are taking extra care during testing and production planning.

Certification and compliance are also part of the process. Because Swellframe includes wireless connectivity and electronics, we need to complete the required testing before shipping. This is a standard part of bringing a connected hardware product to market, but it can introduce delays if any changes are required.

There is also a software and data reliability challenge. Swellframe is designed to show a simple, always-on surf forecast, but behind that simplicity are forecast data sources, connectivity, firmware, and our own forecast logic. We will continue testing across different surf spots, Wi-Fi environments, and usage patterns to make the experience as reliable as possible before fulfillment.

Finally, there is always the risk that timelines move. Our goal is to deliver as promised, but we would rather be honest and ship something we are proud of than rush a product that is not ready. If anything changes, we will communicate clearly with backers and share updates along the way.

We started Swellframe because we wanted a calmer, simpler way to check the waves without reaching for our phones. We are committed to making that real, and we are taking the risks of building a hardware product seriously.
