Environmental commitments

Long-lasting design

We strongly oppose the culture of disposable products. The Trilograph is built to be a lifetime heirloom for your art studio. Its core structure is crafted from premium South American Guatambú Multi-layered engineered wood (Ivory Wood). We specifically chose this high-density material because its cross-grain layered structure prevents warping, cracking, or bending under heavy mechanical pressure, regardless of humidity or climate changes. Furthermore, all our wood is sourced from responsibly managed forests, and we prioritize an "asset-light", optimized manufacturing workflow that minimizes material waste, recycling 100% of our production scraps.

Reusability and recyclability

Our goal is to eliminate single-use plastics from our shipping process. We are designing a modular packaging system that uses recycled cardboard and can be repurposed by the artist as a permanent carrying case for the press. By choosing high-quality wood and steel over plastic components, the main body of the Trilograph is 100% biodegradable (if the varnish is removed) and the rollers are fully recyclable.

Sustainable materials

We prioritize high-grade plywood, a material that is significantly more sustainable than solid hardwoods or energy-intensive plastics. The Guatambú we use is sourced through suppliers that follow responsible forest management practices. Furthermore, our stainless steel components are chosen for their longevity, reducing the need for replacement over the product's lifecycle.

Environmentally friendly factories

To minimize our carbon footprint and ensure total oversight of our environmental impact, we have brought production in-house. Our "Asset Light" manufacturing strategy focuses on a high-efficiency CNC nesting process. This allows us to achieve a "Zero-Waste" design by fitting components so tightly within the plywood sheets that material waste is reduced to the absolute minimum.

Sustainable Distribution

To minimize energy waste, we consolidate our international shipments into "waves". By using FedEx’s optimized global routes from Argentina, we reduce the total number of individual transit points. Additionally, the compact and lightweight design of the Trilograph (only 650 grams) means we consume significantly less fuel and space during air transport compared to traditional heavy-duty presses.

Local Sourcing, Global Impact
One of our biggest environmental advantages is our supply chain verticality. Unlike mass-produced tools that often travel from South America to Asia for processing and then back to the West for sale, we produce everything locally in Argentina. By sourcing our high-grade plywood and stainless steel near our own workshop, we eliminate thousands of miles of maritime and land transport of raw materials, drastically reducing the carbon footprint of every Trilograph before it even reaches your door.Precision Over Waste
While we use professional electric sanding to ensure a high-end finish, our focus on sustainability lies in the Mechanical Efficiency of our workshop. By integrating our own industrial CNC machinery, we control every cut. Our "Zero-Waste" nesting algorithm ensures that we extract the maximum number of parts from every plywood sheet, leaving behind only the smallest fragments of wood, which are then repurposed or recycled locally. By backing this project, you are supporting a direct-to-consumer model that favors local craftsmanship and industrial precision over the wasteful cycles of mass-industrial manufacturing.
