Risks and challenges
Risks & Challenges
Bringing a hardware product from prototype to production is an exciting journey, but it also comes with challenges. We've developed working prototypes and carefully planned our manufacturing process, yet we believe in being transparent with our backers about the potential risks involved.
Product Refinements
RootBoard is still evolving. As we move from prototype to mass production, we may make improvements to the hardware, enclosure, PCB layout, firmware, or accessories to enhance reliability, usability, manufacturability, or overall performance.
The final production version may differ slightly from the prototypes and renders shown on this campaign page. Any changes will always be made with the goal of delivering a better product without compromising the core functionality or vision of RootBoard.

Manufacturing & Supply Chain
Electronic components and manufacturing schedules can sometimes be affected by supplier availability, global logistics, or unexpected production challenges. We have selected experienced manufacturing partners and are working with readily available components whenever possible, but delays can occasionally occur despite careful planning.

Quality Assurance
Every RootBoard will undergo testing and quality inspection before shipping. While this process helps ensure a reliable product, it may also extend production timelines if additional testing or refinements are required. We believe it's better to spend extra time delivering a high-quality product than rushing unfinished hardware.

Software Development
RootBoard is built on Raspberry Pi OS and open-source software. During development, we will continue improving the software experience, documentation, drivers, and setup process. Some software features, user interfaces, or configuration methods may evolve before the final release as we incorporate community feedback and optimize the overall experience.

Open-Source Development
Because RootBoard is an open-source project, we expect both the hardware and software to continue evolving after launch. Community contributions, bug fixes, and feature enhancements may result in improvements beyond the initial release. We're committed to supporting this ecosystem and making updates available whenever possible.

Shipping & Logistics
International shipping may be affected by customs processing, local import regulations, weather conditions, or courier delays. While we'll work closely with our logistics partners to ensure timely delivery, some shipments may take longer than estimated depending on the destination country.

Our Commitment
We have experience developing and launching hardware products and understand the challenges involved in bringing electronics to market. Throughout the campaign and production process, we'll keep our backers informed with regular updates, manufacturing progress, testing milestones, and any changes to the schedule or product.
Our goal is simple: deliver a high-quality, open-source handheld Linux computer that meets the expectations of our community.
Thank you for supporting RootBoard and being part of its journey from prototype to production.

ROOTBOARD - The Open Pocket Linux Computer

A Raspberry Pi-powered handheld Linux computer with a real keyboard, integrated display, speaker, and fully open hardware. Built for makers, developers, cyberdeck enthusiasts, educators, and anyone who believes technology should be open, understandable, and customizable.

A Computer You Can Truly Understand

In a world filled with locked-down devices, proprietary firmware, and hardware that actively discourages modification, RootBoard takes a different path.

RootBoard is a compact handheld Linux computer designed around a simple philosophy:

Technology should be open.

No proprietary keyboard controllers.

No hidden firmware.

No black boxes.

No unnecessary complexity.

Just a Raspberry Pi-powered platform with a real keyboard, integrated display, rechargeable battery support, and software you can inspect, modify, and make your own.
